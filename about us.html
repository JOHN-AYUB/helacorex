A Mother Selling Vegetables in Kisumu

Akeno wakes up at 5 AM, buys produce from the wholesale market, sets up her stall, and sells until evening. She has no formal business training. She doesn't know her profit margin. She can't predict which vegetables will sell faster next week.

Her decisions—how much to buy, what to stock, when to save—are driven by intuition and tradition, not data. One bad week can wipe out months of earnings. A loan could help, but she doesn't know if she can repay it.

Akeno represents 40 million informal entrepreneurs across East Africa.

The Daily Reality

No visibility into which products are actually profitable

Cannot assess whether a loan would help or hurt

No understanding of seasonal patterns affecting her business

No access to the kind of intelligence that large corporations use daily

Our Mission

To democratize business intelligence by making AI-powered guidance accessible, affordable, and actionable for every entrepreneur in emerging markets—regardless of their education level, technical skills, or starting capital.

Our Vision

A world where every small business owner has the same quality of decision-making intelligence as a Fortune 500 CEO—where African entrepreneurs don't just survive, but systematically thrive and build generational wealth.

What Helacore Does

We don't replace human judgment—we augment it. Helacore transforms raw business data into clear, actionable guidance that any entrepreneur can understand and act on.

We Listen

Through simple inputs—voice, text, or automated data feeds—we capture the reality of your business: sales, expenses, inventory, debts, and local market conditions.

We Analyze

Our AI applies predictive analytics, behavioral modeling, and localized data integration to understand your unique business context—down to your neighborhood.

We Guide

We deliver hyper-personalized recommendations in your language—telling you exactly what to do, when to do it, and what to expect, in plain terms you can act on immediately.

Our Values

These aren't just words on a wall. They shape every product decision, every hire, and every partnership we make.

Accessibility First

If a vegetable seller with a basic phone can't use it, we haven't done our job. We design for the most constrained user, and everyone benefits.

Privacy by Design

Your business data is yours. We never sell it, share it individually, or compromise it. Market intelligence comes only from anonymized aggregation.

Local Context, Global Intelligence

We build for African markets first. That means understanding mobile money, informal supply chains, seasonal patterns, and linguistic diversity.

Our Journey

From a napkin sketch in Nairobi to 2.4 million businesses across Kenya.

2021 — The Spark

Wanjiku and Kamau meet at a Nairobi tech hub. They share a frustration: the most powerful AI in the world is being used to optimize ads, not to help a mother feed her children through her small business.

2022 — Prototype & Pilot

Built the first prototype with 50 kiosk owners in Thika. The average pilot user saw a 22% profit increase within 3 months. Secured pre-seed funding from TechStars Nairobi.

2023 — Scale & Product-Market Fit

Launched voice interface in Swahili. Grew to 500K users across 20 counties. Partnered with M-Pesa for automatic transaction syncing. Raised $4.2M Series A.

2024 — National Impact

Reached 2.4M active users across all 47 Kenyan counties. Launched Marketplace Connector and What-If Simulation Engine. Recognized by World Bank as a top fintech innovation.

2025 — Pan-African Expansion

Expanding to Tanzania, Uganda, and Nigeria. Launching AI University Builder for institutional partners. Building the infrastructure for 10M African businesses by 2027.
